What's actually at stake

A governor isn't one vote among 100 — they run the state. Here's what the winner controls.

The budget
Proposes Ohio's two-year budget — where school funding, Medicaid, and property-tax relief all start.
The trifecta question
Republicans hold supermajorities in both chambers. Ramaswamy would complete one-party control; Acton would be the only check on it.
Redistricting seat
The governor sits on the Ohio Redistricting Commission — a direct vote on the next round of maps.
Agencies & universities
Appoints agency heads and university trustees — and the two disagree sharply on higher ed's future.

Same crisis, opposite fixes

Both campaigns are about affordability. Their playbooks barely overlap:

ACTON'S PITCH
"People in this state are struggling. They are doing everything right." Cut costs directly — schools, prescriptions, utilities, Medicaid — plus a working-families tax cut.
RAMASWAMY'S PITCH
"Make Ohio the most affordable, opportunity-rich state in America." Cut costs by shrinking: income taxes toward zero, capped property taxes, more natural gas.

The backstory

Ohio was supposed to be safe Republican territory: Trump won it by 11 in 2024, and no Democrat has won the governorship since 2006. But term-limited Mike DeWine leaves an open seat, and the race has tightened to a coin flip — largely because women voters have swung hard toward Acton while men stay with Ramaswamy.

The attack lines write themselves. Republicans call Acton "Dr. Lockdown" for the 2020 school and business closures she signed — though DeWine, who endorsed Ramaswamy, says those calls "were made by the governor." Acton casts Ramaswamy as a billionaire trying to buy the state — "No, Mr. Ramaswamy, you cannot buy Ohio" — and his idea of shutting "subpar" universities like Akron and Kent State is her favorite target.

The stakes are structural: Republicans hold supermajorities in the legislature, so a Ramaswamy win completes one-party control, while Acton would be the lone check on it. With $50M+ already raised and a $25M attack-ad blitz underway, this is on track to be the most expensive governor's race in Ohio history.
