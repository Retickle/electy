Their big promises

PELTOLA'S TOP 3
Make Alaska affordable
Her campaign centerpiece: bring down costs for groceries, energy, housing, and health care.
Fish, family, freedom
Protect salmon runs and fishing livelihoods; defend abortion rights from federal interference.
Alaska-first independence
A Blue Dog record of bucking her party — she backed the Willow oil project and gun rights.
SULLIVAN'S TOP 3
Unleash Alaska's resources
Open more land for oil, gas, and mining; champion the Alaska LNG pipeline project.
Military strength in the Arctic
Grow Alaska's bases and missile defense — the state hosts a fifth of U.S. military assets.
Seniority with the majority
A dozen years of committee posts, backed by Sen. Murkowski and Alaska Native corporations.

Their stories

MARY PELTOLA
1973Born in Anchorage; grew up fishing on the Kuskokwim River; Yup'ik, raised in Bethel.
1999Elected to the Alaska House at 25; served a decade, then managed inter-tribal fisheries.
2022Won Alaska's House seat — beating Sarah Palin — becoming the first Alaska Native in Congress; narrowly lost it in 2024.
2026After heavy courting by national Democrats, entered the Senate race in January with an affordability-first campaign.
DAN SULLIVAN
1964Born in Ohio; Marine Corps officer, still a colonel in the Reserve; moved to Alaska in the 1990s.
2009Alaska attorney general, then commissioner of natural resources under Gov. Parnell.
2014Unseated Democratic Sen. Mark Begich; re-elected in 2020 by 13 points.
2026Seeking a third term with endorsements from Sen. Murkowski and Alaska Native regional corporations.
