The backstory

Here's the short version: no Democrat has won statewide in Texas since 1994, and this seat has been Republican for decades. It was supposed to be Sen. John Cornyn's to defend — until Ken Paxton, the state's hard-right attorney general, challenged him in the primary and won, turning a safe seat into an open brawl.

Paxton comes with baggage: he was impeached by his own party's Texas House over corruption allegations (the Senate acquitted him), spent nearly a decade under securities-fraud charges before a 2024 deal ended them, and led the failed lawsuit to overturn the 2020 election. National Republicans privately worried he'd be the one Republican who could actually lose Texas.

On the other side, Democrats nominated James Talarico — a 37-year-old former middle-school teacher and seminary student whose viral videos about faith and politics made him a small-dollar fundraising phenomenon. He's betting a preacher's message and an anti-corruption pitch can do what no Texas Democrat has done in three decades.

And the polls say it's working, so far: the latest NYT/Siena survey has Talarico up 48-46 — inside the margin of error, but shocking for Texas. Forecasters call it a toss-up. If Democrats actually flip this seat, the fight for the Senate is probably over.
