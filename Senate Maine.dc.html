The backstory

Here's the short version: Susan Collins has been Maine's senator since 1997 and has never lost. She's the only Republican senator left defending a state that voted Democratic in 2024, which makes this seat one of Democrats' best pickup chances anywhere. Everyone expected a marquee fight — most assumed against Governor Janet Mills.

Instead, the story of this race is Graham Platner — an oyster farmer and Marine veteran nobody had heard of a year ago. He launched in August 2025 running as an anti-oligarchy populist, picked up endorsements from Bernie Sanders and Elizabeth Warren, and built such a grassroots wave that Mills dropped out of the primary in April.

It hasn't been smooth. Platner's campaign got hit with a string of personal controversies — a Nazi-linked tattoo he says he didn't understand, old online posts, and reporting on his personal conduct that had national Democrats openly nervous. He denied the worst of it, refused to drop out, and Maine's primary voters shrugged: he won the nomination easily on June 9.

Now the polls show a genuine toss-up, with most surveys giving Platner a slight edge — and everyone remembers Collins trailed every public poll in 2020 and won by nine. Her case: her Appropriations chairmanship delivers for Maine. His case: the election is a referendum on Trump, and Collins enables him. Control of the Senate could come down to this one.
